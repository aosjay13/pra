PRA iRacing Rulebook

Note: Caution rulings listed below will be overruled by intentional contact rules if the incident is deemed intentional. Any incidents that are deemed a "netcode" incident will not be given any of the penalties listed below.

Caution Rules

If race control needs extra time to determine the incident, more caution laps will be added.

Rookie Series Incident Rules

  • 1st incident causing a caution: No action taken
  • 2nd incident: EOL (End of Line) penalty
  • 3rd incident: 1 lap penalty
  • 4th incident: DQ (Disqualification)

Pro Series Incident Rules

  • 1st incident: EOL penalty
  • 2nd incident: EOL penalty
  • 3rd incident: 1 lap penalty
  • 4th incident: DQ

Unintentional Contact

Will be reviewed as requested through the submitted incident form. The form will be in the Discord with all the steps of how to submit as well as deadlines. Any infractions deemed unintentional will be reviewed by the race officials and Board of Directors.

Unintentional contact could include:

  • Bump and Runs
  • Corner diving of 2 car lengths or more
  • Not backing out of locked corner panels (bumper locks)

Intentional Contact

The Board of Directors will make the decision of penalty severity. Intentional contact includes:

  • Avoidable pit maneuvers (intentionally turning into another vehicle’s corner panel)
  • Avoidable dive bombs (0 braking into a corner, targeting another driver)
  • Driving through competitors on starts/restarts
  • Pushing people off the track (with ill intent)
Penalty: 1 full week suspension of the main series. If the infraction occurs on championship week, the suspension carries over to the following season.

Race Manipulation

Bump Starts

A bump start is when two or more drivers time the green of the race intentionally to gain an advantage over other competitors. If officials call a bump start after review, drivers will be given a DQ for that race. Repeat infractions will receive additional penalties up to a league ban.

Team Tactics

Team tactics include but are not limited to:

  • Blocking for a fellow racer
  • Causing a caution to better yourself or a teammate's position
  • Forcing people into a worse line to better a teammate's position

Enforcing Infractions

For infractions to be enforced, you must send in an incident form found in the Discord. THESE MUST BE FILLED IN BY THE DAY AFTER THE RACE. At midnight on the day after the races, no incident form will be considered. If the form is not working, reach out to a member of the BOARD OF DIRECTORS.

Series Specific Rules

Each series may have additional rules in a separate rulebook (e.g., start types, fast repairs, specific Blue Flag rules).

Extra Rules

Blue Flag Rules

Refer to your series-specific rulebook for in-depth rules. Generally, Blue Flags are a warning that faster drivers are approaching. Be predictable. Faster drivers must be patient and pass cleanly; slower drivers are allowed on the track.

Legal Racing Surface

At least two wheels must always be on the racing surface. Cutting the track results in grid penalties for the first offense and a stop & go penalty for the second offense.

Voice Communication

  • Do not abuse other drivers. You will be muted after one offense.
  • Repeat abuse offenses result in a ban.
  • Being disruptive will get you warned up to 2 times, then server muted.
  • Repeat offenders will be banned indefinitely or permanently.

Discord Usage During Races

It is not mandatory to be in Discord during races, but there must be a way for officials to communicate with you (even if text). Refrain from excessively joining/leaving channels.

Paint Scheme Rules

Paint schemes must be professional. Meme schemes are not allowed. Series-specific contingency logos are required. Ensure your paint number matches your sign-up number.

Age & Conduct

16+ Requirement

You must be 16 or older to participate. Immaturity in sign-up, Discord, or on-track can result in removal.

PRA iRacing Officials

  • Must be 18 or older.
  • Must be vetted by a Board Member (sitting in at least 1 season).
  • Each series needs at least 3 officials to operate.
  • Officials displaying anger or toxicity will be removed immediately.

Simplified Code of Conduct

  • No political talk
  • No religious talk
  • Do not make racial remarks
  • Do not make bigoted remarks
  • Do not bully other members
  • Absolutely no toxicity
  • Do not spam the Discord chat

This includes your PROFILE BIO in Discord. Failure to adhere can lead to suspensions/bans.

PRA Sunday Showdown Series Info/Rules

Series Information

  • League ID: #11855 – You will not be accepted unless entry fee is paid
  • Weather: Realistic - Practice will be set for 7 pm est in game, Qualifying 8 pm est, Heat Races 8:30 pm, Feature 9 pm
  • Schedule of events:
    • Lobby opens at 7:00 pm EST.
    • Practice: 7:00 pm to 7:15 pm
    • Qualify: 7:15 pm to 7:25 pm
    • Heat Races: 7:25 pm
    • Feature: to follow

Points

  • Qualifying: Top 5 earn points starting at 5 and going down to 1
  • Heat Races: Top 10 earn points starting at 10 going down to 1
  • Consolation: No points
  • Feature: 30 cars make the field, Earning points from 30 down to 1
  • Bonus points: laps led, most laps led

Series Specific Rules

Note: All Standard PRA iRacing Rules still apply.

Starts

  • All starts are on the leader- start zone is anywhere from the entry of turn 4 to the auto green flag.
  • Jump Starts: will result in a 1 row penalty no warnings given
  • Must be "bunched up" (no more than 1 car length between cars) before midpoint of back straight
  • Leader Must maintain pace speed +/- 5 mph (ex: 35 mph pace speed 30 to 40 mph is legal) field should match leader pace.
  • No rapid acceleration/deceleration. Must maintain constant speed off of turn 2 with 1 to go
Penalties: Will count as "Jump Start" 1 row penalty no warning. Penalty will be assessed at the next yellow or end of race.

Track Hazards

  • If the official deems you a track hazard you will be asked to park it for the race
  • Hazards can be anything from network issues (excessive blinking), to car control issues.

Black Flags

  • Black flags given by iRacing will be cleared at Officials Discretion.
  • We understand that sometimes iRacing drops one on you for something stupid, however if you make a mistake (passing before the start/finish, speeding in pits, ect.) we will not clear those

Practice Rules

Practice is not a demolition derby. If you treat it as such you will be removed before the race begins.

Incidents

  • Official assigns "fault" for the incident
  • Driver at fault will receive an EOL
  • 3 EOL penalties result in auto DQ
  • Intentional wrecking, deemed by official, will result in auto DQ

Officials

  • Official’s rulings are ultimate and final during the race. Do not argue the calls during the race.
  • Incident report form: If you have an incident you feel the official missed, or a call that the official got wrong please fill out the incident report form linked in the Gladiator info channel and it will be reviewed by a neutral party of iRacing Officials and Board of Directors members.

*All rules are subject to change! Rule updates will be communicated to drivers via Sunday Showdown series Discord chat channels*

Sunset Bend Street Stock Road Course Challenge

Season 2 Rulebook Briefing

1. Introduction

1.1 About the Championship

Operated by the Prodigy Racing Association (PRA), the Sunset Bend Street Stock Road Course Challenge is a road racing championship designed for competitive, fair, and accessible sim racing across all skill levels, emphasizing a low-drama environment and a supportive culture. This multi-round series utilizes the iRacing Street Stock car on a diverse calendar of North American and international road circuits.

1.2 Objectives

  • Deliver clean, hard, competitive racing.
  • Maintain a friendly, supportive, low-drama community.
  • Provide a structured championship with playoffs and team competition.
  • Offer free entry and free broadcast coverage courtesy of PRA.

1.3 Governance

The championship is organized and moderated by the PRA administration team. The PRA Competition Code applies at all times.

2. Season Format

2.1 Weekly Schedule (Every Thursday)

  • Practice: 8:00 PM EST (1 hour)
  • Qualifying: 9:00 PM EST (10 Min Open session)
  • Race 1: 20-minute points-paying race
  • Race 2: 20-minute points-paying race with a full-field inversion based on Race 1 results

2.2 Sessions

  • All sessions are held in PRA “Sunset Bend” league. [ID # 13295]
  • Weather and track conditions follow iRacing’s dynamic settings unless otherwise announced. [This vehicle does not have rain tires]

2.3 Communications

Communications will be made mainly in PRA Discord followed by race day reminders via iRacing’s UI league announcements page.

3. Season Structure

3.1 Regular Season (Rounds 1–8)

  • 1. Daytona Road Course - January 15th
  • 2. Charlotte Roval - January 22nd
  • 3. Oulton Park - January 29th
  • 4. Oran Park - February 5th
  • 5. Navarra Medium - February 12th
  • 6. Okayama Full - February 19th
  • 7. Road America - February 26th
  • 8. Watkins Glen - March 5th

3.2 Chase Playoffs (Rounds 9–12)

The final four races form the Chase Championship:

  • 9. Sebring International Raceway - March 12th
  • 10. VIR Full Course - March 19th
  • 11. Rudskogen Motorsenter - March 26th
  • 12. Adelaide Street Circuit (Finale) - April 2nd

4. Points System

4.1 Regular Season Points

The current points system applies for all races, including bonus points defined within that system:

  • 45pts to the Winner, 39 to second, 38 to third and -1 from there down to 40.
  • 3 Bonus Points for Pole Position.

4.2 Chase Qualification

  • After Round 8, the top 10 drivers in the standings will qualify for the “Chase”.
  • Chase drivers have their points reset to 1000.

4.3 Post-Season Bonus Points

After the regular season concludes (but before the Chase begins):

  • +5 points per regular season win
  • +1 point per regular season pole position

These are added to each Chase driver’s reset total.

4.4 “Chase” Champion

  • Points accrued in Rounds 9–12 determine the overall champion.
  • The driver with the highest total after Adelaide is the Season 2 Champion.

5. Teams Championship

5.1 Team Composition

  • Teams may be formed at any time, but points only start counting once registered.
  • Maximum 4 drivers per team.
  • Only points awarded race by race count towards the team championship, no chase reset values or bonus points are considered.

6. Sporting Regulations

6.1 Starts & Restarts [If Necessary]

  • All starts are “Go on Green.”
  • Restarts can occur in the event of catastrophic network / server error.
  • iRacing controls restarts unless otherwise announced.

6.2 Track Limits

  • Governed by iRacing’s automated system.
  • Drivers are expected not to exploit leniency or intentionally benefit from off-tracks or slowdowns. Penalties will be considered for repeat violations.

6.3 Incident Limits

  • No incident limit for Season 2.
  • Excessive or reckless contact may be penalized post-race.

6.4 Overtaking Guidelines

Standard “Nascar”- Styled racing etiquette applies.

6.5 Blue Flags

Drivers being lapped must maintain predictable racing lines and allow faster traffic to pass safely.

6.6 Disconnects & Technical Issues

  • Events continue unless the lobby itself fails.
  • No restarts for individual disconnects or incidents.

7. Conduct & Stewarding

7.1 Behavior Expectations

All drivers must follow the PRA Code of Conduct at all times, including:

  • Respectful communication
  • Zero tolerance for harassment or abuse
  • No public arguments during or after races

7.2 Protest System

  • Protests must be submitted via the PRA Discord.
  • Stewards may review incidents proactively if necessary.

7.3 Penalties

Possible penalties include:

  • Time penalties
  • Points deductions
  • Race bans
  • Removal from the championship (for serious or repeated offences)

8. Media, Broadcasting & Promotion

8.1 Broadcast Coverage

PRA & Grid Finder provide:

  • Live race broadcasts [PRA]
  • Highlights packages [PRA & Grid Finder]
  • Media reels and recap posts [Grid Finder]

9. Entry Requirements

9.1 Eligibility

  • Active iRacing subscription
  • Street Stock car [Free]
  • All required tracks (7 Free, 5 paid)
  • Join the PRA Discord and Register to the championship on GridFinder.com, mark your attendance.
  • Acceptance & acknowledgement of all rules and codes of conduct

9.2 Registration

Registration is handled via Grid Finder website or PRA Discord. Drivers must register before competing in points-paying races.

10. Summary

Season 2 offers an accessible yet competitive racing environment with a playoff format, a full-season teams championship, free broadcasting, and a community-first atmosphere. The Sunset Bend & PRA team aim to deliver racing that is intense, rewarding, and enjoyable for every participant.

Asphalt Assault Series Rulebook

Series Officials

  • Series Director: Justin May (Phoenix)
  • Officiating Crew: Shannon Milliken (Flashbang)

Race Settings

  • Time Limit: 1 Hour | Practice Length: 25 Minutes
  • Qualifying Laps: 2
  • Race Laps: Varies Per Track
  • Start Zone: ❌
  • Fast Repairs: 1
  • Max Incidents: N/A
  • Incident Warning: ❌
  • Qualifying Conduct Scrutiny: Strict
  • Starting Type: Rolling
  • Restart Type: Double-File Back
  • G/W/C Attempts: 2
  • Full Course Cautions: ✔
  • Lucky Dog: ✔
  • Wave Arounds: ✔
  • Repeat Cautions - Single File Restart: ✔
  • Damage: ✔

Advanced Settings

  • Hardcore Level: Disallow all driving aids (but clutch assist OK)
  • License Range: Rookie 1.0 > PRO/WC 4.0
  • Fixed Setup: Provided by iRacing unless track has no default.

Practice & Racing Etiquette

Practice: Drivers must maintain control. Rude driving or intentional wrecking in practice will result in disqualification from the event.

Clean Racing: A maximum of 3 EOL penalties are allowed before disqualification.

Aggressive Driving: Do not drive through others just because you are faster. Causing an incident due to impatience will result in an EOL or DQ.

Starts & Restarts

  • Leader Control: Starts/Restarts are at the discretion of the leader.
  • 2nd Place: Must not jump the start. Passing the leader before the line = Drive Through Penalty.
  • Lane Integrity: Do not pass the driver in your line before the start/finish line unless avoiding an emergency.
  • Issues: If you have an issue (missed shift, etc.), pull to the apron immediately.

Incident Rules

  • At Fault: Drivers deemed "At Fault" (including self-spins) receive an EOL.
  • Strike Out: 4th EOL penalty = DQ.
  • Aggressive Driving: If a driver gets an EOL and then drives over others on the restart, they will be DQ'd.
  • DO NOT ARGUE: Arguing with officials in race chat results in immediate ejection.

Points Penalties

  • 1st Fault: EOL (No points penalty)
  • 2nd Fault: EOL + (-10 pts)
  • 3rd Fault: DQ (0 pts for race)
  • Intentional Crash: 1 Race Suspension + 0 pts

Probation Rules

If a driver has been DQ'd, they are on probation for 1 race.

  • Drivers on probation get -10 points per fault (No "freebie" first EOL).
  • 3rd fault on probation = DQ.
  • DQ while on probation = Suspension until start of next season.

Number Rules

Maximum of 3 drivers can have the same number (e.g., #3). First driver gets #3. Second must use a variation (e.g., 03 or 3a). Third must use another variation (e.g., 003 or 3JTM).

Last Place Points (LPP)

Drivers can post "LPP" in the Discord channel for races they miss. They will receive points as if they finished last in every session of that event.

Voice Chat Rules

Race-related comms only. No personal conversations. Toxicity or arguing with officials results in muting and potential post-race penalties.

PRA Wreckfest Rules

Unintentional Contact

Will be reviewed as requested through the submitted incident form. The form will be in the discord with all the steps of how to submit as well as when they need to be submitted by. Any infractions that are unintentional will be reviewed by the race officials and Board of Directors. They will make the decision of time penalties and/or (in rare cases) race suspensions.

Unintentional contact could include any of the following plus any that the Board deems infraction worthy.

  • Bump and Runs
  • Corner diving of 2 or more car lengths
  • Not backing out of locked corner panels (bumper locks)

Intentional Contact

Intentional contact includes,

  • Avoidable pit moves (intentionally turning into another vehicle’s corner panel)
  • Avoidable Dive bombs (0 braking in corner, targeting another driver)
  • Driving backwards
  • Pushing people off the track (with ill intent)

New to Season 22! We have received complaints of drivers leaving races mid match causing confusion and potential wrecks due to lag and cars disappearing from the track.

Our new procedure for leaving mid race is listed below:

  1. Pull over to a safe place off the track not impeding other drivers.
  2. Inform the race official you are leaving the race.

Only after the completion of steps 1 & 2 may a driver leave the race.

FAILURE TO FOLLOW THE STEPS ABOVE WILL FORFEIT YOUR POINTS FOR THAT RACE WEEK. REPEAT OFFENSES CAN LEAD TO GREATER CONSEQUENCES.

Cautions

We will be on a two tally system, and will be racing back to the start/finish line when a caution is called. EACH RACE WILL HAVE A DEDICATED RACE OFFICIAL. The official will be the only person able to call cautions as well as restarts in the race.

When a caution is called and you race back to the line you will start wherever you cross the line, even if you are involved in the tally causing caution.

Tallies

A tally occurs when 1 car is spun in a racing incident involving 2 or more drivers. This means that if you are by yourself and spin or overcook a corner, a tally will not be called!

IF BOTH CARS ARE SPUN, that will be considered 2 tallies and a caution will be thrown out.

NEW TO SEASON 18: TALLIES CAN BE CALLED ON THE FIRST LAP OF THE RACE AND ON RESTARTS

Tie Breakers

In the event of a tie in points at the end of the season the tie breaker will go to most race wins. If both drivers are still tied the tie breaker will go to laps lead.

All drivers who sign up for LPP will receive the same amount of points.

There will be a channel to sign up for LPP in the discord.

[ Official Handbook ]

Introduction

Welcome to Prodigy Racing Association, where we aim to provide a fun, professional racing environment. PRA is currently hosting multiple racing leagues over multiple different platforms including PlayStation Network, PC, and Xbox.

This handbook will oversee multiple aspects of PRA and is subject to change at any time. We will do our best to inform league members when an update is made.

All members are encouraged to contact a league official with any ideas that may improve the quality of the league.

This is the Official Handbook for all of the Prodigy Racing Association racing leagues. Each series will have their own Official Rulebook. In this handbook you will find everything you need to hit the ground running and start your PRA career.

[Rule 1] Chat Rooms/Messaging
[Article 1] Communication

Prodigy Racing Association uses the message service ‘Discord’ as its only form of communication.

[Article 2] Conduct

League members are expected to maintain clean, respectful conversation in all chats and races.

  • [Sec. 1] Any derogatory, degrading comments made towards other league members will be reviewed and disciplinary action will be taken.
  • [Sec. 2] PRA will not tolerate harassment of any kind.
    • [Sub-Sec. 1] Officials have full authority to deem any action as potential harassment.
  • [Sec. 3] PRA will ensure a professional and friendly racing environment.
[Article 3] Race Chat

During the race, drivers should only talk about race related topics.

  • [Sec. 1] It is important to keep chatting to a minimum during the races.
  • [Sec. 2] Minimum chatting during the races will lead to a better officiating of the race.
[Article 4] Detrimental Actions
  • [Sec 1.] The PRA Board of Directors have FULL authority to deem any actions, made by a league member, detrimental.
    • [Sub-Sec. 1] Actions can range from in-race to social media posts.
    • [Sub-Sec. 2] Arguing with an official ruling during the race is deemed detrimental to the league.
[Rule 2] Aggressive Driving
[Article 1] Zero Tolerance

PRA holds a ZERO tolerance policy towards over aggressive driving.

  • [Sec. 1] League Officials understand racing to win and although there is a gray area between aggressive and over aggressive driving, drivers are expected to attempt to avoid such scenarios whenever possible.
  • [Sec. 2] PRA Officials have the authority to penalize any driver they think is driving over aggressively.
[Article 2] Intentional Wrecking
Intentionally spinning or wrecking other league members will result in point deductions, suspensions, or other disciplinary action deemed acceptable by league officials.
[Article 3] Complaints

If a driver wishes to file a complaint about a driver, they must submit a complaint.

  • [Sec. 1] How to submit a complaint
    • [Sub-Sec. 1] Submit proof to PRA Official using the ‘PRA Complaint Form’ via Discord of the incident.
    • [Sub-Sec. 2] Verbal complaints will be taken into consideration, but the situation will unlikely be fixed without proof.
[Rule 3] Penalties
[Article 1] Assessment

A penalty can be assessed to a driver if they race too aggressively, intentionally hindering the racing, or is disrespectful to a PRA Official or driver.

[Article 2] Severity

Penalties can range from probation to expulsion from the league (depending on severity of the driver’s actions).

[Article 3] During Race

All penalties during the race are non-negotiable.

  • [Sec. 1] If a driver fails to comply with PRA Officials, they will be removed from the race and further disciplinary actions will be administered.
[Article 4] Appeals

If you are assessed a penalty and do not agree, you can appeal the penalty after both races are concluded.

[Article 5] How to Appeal
  • [Sec. 1] In order to appeal a penalty, you must private message ‘to the series officials’ on DISCORD saying you appeal the penalty.
  • [Sec. 2] An appeal can contain the following:
    • [Sub-Sec. 1] A request to chat with PRA Officials to talk about the incident.
    • [Sub-Sec. 2] A Discord message with video/photographic proof.
    • [Sub-Sec. 3] Appointing a witness to talk to PRA Officials.
[Article 6] Successful Appeals

If a driver WINS the appeal, the driver’s penalty will be reversed, and compensation will be awarded if needed.
